# $${\color{blue}Palindrome \space Number}$$
## $${\color{orange}Concept \space explanantion:}$$
A $${\color{blue}Palindrome \space Number}$$ is simply a number that is identical to its reversed form, $${\color{pink} for \space an \space example:}$$ 12321,<br>Digits:  1 2 3 2 1<br>Reversed order:  1 2 3 2 1<br><br>
So this is a $${\color{blue}Palindrome \space Number}$$.<br><br>
$${\color{pink}example \space of}$$$${\color{red}non-}$$$${\color{blue}Palindrome-Number}$$$${\color{pink}:}$$


Integer number:               1 2 3 2 1<br>
Their digital place:          0 1 2 3 4<br>
The reversed integer number:  1 2 3 2 1<br>
Their reversed digital place: 4 3 2 1 0<br>
